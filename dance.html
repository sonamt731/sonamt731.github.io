Growing up, I used to participate in competitions on both the regional and national levels. The stage went from one of my biggest fears to one of the places I feel most confident.
 
   
To date, one of my proudest personal achievements has been the completion of my Arangetram (indian classical dance graduation). Bharatanatyam is the oldest classical dance traditon in India and is known for its rigor, story telling, and purity. With its origins dating back more than 2000 years, bharatanatyam stands as an integral part of India's history and values. I started bharatanatyam when I was 7 and have trained for over 11 years in the art form. It was an honor to complete the 5 hour live showcase for my friends and family and stand beside my guru (instructor) to recieve my graduation plaque. As I continue my dance journey, it has been a continuous strive of mine to incorporate this unique and intricate dance form, preserving and passing on bharatanatyam to the next generation.
     
The summer of 2017, the Indian Oscars Association (IIFA) held a worldwide contest in which participants could submit a short video dancing and people would vote for which dancers they wanted to attend the award show. In hearing about this once in a life time opportunity, I decided to submit a video. I could not decide which form of dance or what song.. so I submitted a fusion dance incorporating as many different forms as I could.
